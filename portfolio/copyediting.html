Copyediting

For two years, I was a full-time copyeditor for three university presses, as well as a handful of authors and journals, for a total of more than 1.5 million words. For most books, I was hired by the press and in contact with the author throughout the process. Most of my clients asked for Chicago style, though a few had house styles.

Beyond learning Chicago style and the manuscript editorial process, this experience got me thinking about editorial tools. Most authors and presses in the humanities still depend on Microsoft Word, but after the tenth time Word crashed on a project with more than a hundred margin comments and more than a hundred thousand words, I realized that Word really was not built for book editing and may never be. When I learned about projects like the University of California Press’s Editoria and the MIT Press’s PubPub, I became curious about the future of editing tools.
