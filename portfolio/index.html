Bio

I am a humanist with digital skills and a strong interest in university presses and university libraries. I hold an MA in English from Georgetown University, and I was a Fulbright scholar to Poland, where I taught English at the University of Silesia.

At the University of Michigan School of Information, I assessed libraries’ support for qualitative researchers, curated the metadata for several hundred books being digitized under an NEH/Mellon grant, and conducted user experience research on a complex resource platform serving academic libraries. As a Digital Strategies and Innovation Intern at Harvard Library, I developed and tested features for Harvard Digital Collections, a newly launched open digital archive.

This website was designed with inspiration from Harvard Library.
