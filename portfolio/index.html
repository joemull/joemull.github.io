Bio

Joseph Muller is a humanist and technologist with a strong interest in university presses and university libraries. He holds an MA in English from Georgetown University and is currently earning a Master of Science in Information at the University of Michigan. With an academic background in comparative literature and writing, international experience as a Fulbright scholar, and professional training in editing, library science, and web development, he wants to break down barriers of place and access, opening the humanities to new writers and readers.

At the University of Michigan School of Information, Muller assessed libraries’ support for qualitative researchers, curated the metadata for several hundred books being digitized under an NEH/Mellon grant, and conducted user experience research on a complex resource platform serving academic libraries. As a Digital Strategies and Innovation Intern at Harvard Library, he developed and tested new features for Harvard Digital Collections, the university’s flagship open digital archive.
